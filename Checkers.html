Rules:
Moves are allowed only on the dark squares, so pieces always move diagonally.
Single Pawns are always limited to forward moves (toward the opponent, as they are positioned at the start).
A Pawn making a non-eating move (not involving a jump) may move only one square.
A piece making a capturing move (a jump) leaps over one of the opponent's pieces, landing in a straight diagonal line on the other side.
Only one piece may be captured in a single jump.
When a Pawn reaches the furthest row from its starting position it is crowned and becomes a King.
Kings are limited to moving diagonally but may move both forward and backward.
After a king eats a piece he "land" where he wants on the same diagonal.
You cannot eat twice in a single turn.
The game ends when one of the players loses all their Pawns.
To move press once on the piece you wish to move and once when you want it to go.
If the piece does not move, the play you made is not allowed.
To make the computer move, press the button below.
To play instead of the computer, just play normally.
